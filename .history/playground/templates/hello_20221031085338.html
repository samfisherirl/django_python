Geo3D

Welcome to my corner on the internet! My name is Ulf Jälmbrant and live in a very small town in Sweden called Lödöse. I have a master degree from CHALMERS in integrated electronic system design.

Geo3D supports DirectX 9-12 which wouldn't be possible without ReShade's Api. In the background is my disassembler fix and my assembler. The first part of 2022 laid the groundwork for my recent quick progress in ReShade. Released on my patreon page here.

There are three values that affect 3D: Convergence ScreenSize Separation Setting the right ScreenSize is very important. If you reduce separation% you will avoid having your eye diverge. Convergence does not work in all games and I don't know why. Convergence is key to making the presentation less flat. Turning off input 3D allows you to easily spot where virtual screen makes things overlap.

Instructions

Start with downloading the latest addon ReShade from reshade.me. Then you can use 7-zip to unpack reshade. Extract Geo3D and place reshade into the folders. After moving reshade into the Geo3D folders rename both reshade32.dll and reshade64.dll into dxgi.dll for each folder. For DirectX 9 games this has to be renamed again to d3d9.dll or for some DirectX 12 games into d3d12.dll. Be careful with existing ReShade installations. Copy the Geo3D section into the existing reshade.ini. You probably want to keep your existing ReShade preset and need to get 3DToElse.fx into a suitable place.
